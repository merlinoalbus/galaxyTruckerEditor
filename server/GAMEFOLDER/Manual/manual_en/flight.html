The Flight


The flight consists of certain number of adventure cards (in this case 8).


The flight track shows the relative positions of the ships during the flight. All ships are constantly moving toward the destination at the same speed. Along the way, certain adventures may cause them to change their relative positions.

The flight is finished after all adventure cards are revealed.

Views

Click a player's avatar to view his or her ship.


Or just click on the ship directly.


In the ship view, you can click the magnifying glass icon to return to the flight-track view.

Info Panel

The info panel is available when you are zoomed in on any player's ship. Click the tab at the bottom of your screen to bring up the info panel.

 

The info panel gives you lots of useful information:

  • The trucker's name.
  • The class of the ship. (Ships with higher roman numerals are bigger.)
  • The number of credits earned from adventures on this flight.
  • The insurance – the limit on how much the player might pay for lost components.
  • Number of components lost along the way.
  • The amount the player will have to pay for those lost components.
  • The rewards for finishing the flight. (Players who finish ahead get more credits.)
  • The reward for having the fewest exposed connectors.
  • The total monetary value of all goods on board.
  • The number of crew members.
  • The number of batteries .
  • The engine strength.
  • The cannon strength.
  • The number of exposed connectors.
  • And "Give up on this flight" button.

Click the info panel to slide it back to the bottom of the screen.

Adventure Cards

The events of the flight are determined by the adventure cards, which are located in the upper left corner of the screen. The cards shown here are all level I. Level II and level III adventures are tougher and offer better rewards. On flights with higher-level adventure cards, you will have a bigger ship.

The deck gives you a hint about the card that is coming next. On a level I flight, all these cards will be level I, but on higher-level flights, the cards will be a mix of levels. If you see a III on top of the deck, you know that the next adventure will bring big rewards or big trouble. Maybe both.

Click on the deck to see what the next adventure will be!

What Can Happen to You During the Flight

The adventure cards provide a number of challenges and opportunities.

Gaining and Losing Flight Days

Some adventures can cause you to lose flight days. Cards that offer rewards have a number in the lower right corner. That is the number of days you will lose if you take the reward. You also might lose flight days when you are trying to navigate a Combat Zone.

When you lose flight days, your ship moves backward on the flight track. Two ships cannot occupy the same space, so a ship moving backward will leapfrog over ships behind it:

Similarly, when a ship gains flight days, it moves forward, leapfrogging over ships ahead of it. Your chance to gain flight days is Open Space.

Sometimes you want to be ahead and sometimes you don't.

The leader always gets first choice of any rewards. These rewards come with a penalty of lost flight days. Sometimes you might want to reject a reward to keep yourself ahead of other players.

Players who are ahead have to fight enemies first. This is good if you can defeat the enemies, but bad if they can defeat you. Players who are ahead also lose ties in Combat Zones. Sometimes, it might be advantageous to be behind other players.

If you survive to the end of the flight, your finishing bonus will be better if you are farther ahead. So it's always good to be ahead after the last adventure is done.

Gaining Goods

When an adventure card enables you to load goods onto your ship, you may drag them from the dialog box and drop them in your cargo containers. Or you can simply click them and they will be moved to your ship automatically.

Try to keep your most valuable goods in cargo holds that are least likely to get destroyed during the flight.

Any time you take an opportunity to gain goods, you can also rearrange the goods you already have by dragging them to new containers.

Excess goods have to be dumped into space. You may drag goods from your ship back to the dialog box or simply click the goods in the containers and they will be moved automatically.

The price list will help you decide what to keep and what to dump:



Red goods are the most valuable, but they are hazardous materials and must be kept in red containers, not white ones. Red containers can also hold yellow, green, or blue goods.

As long as you have enough space, you should take everything you can get, even if you have to put cheap goods in red containers. When you load new goods, you can always rearrange or dump any amount of cargo.

When you are satisfied with your arrangement of the cargo, click the icon to indicate that you are ready to continue.

Losing Goods

If a card requires you to give up goods, you will lose the most valuable goods first. The attackers will take these goods automatically and you will not get to choose where they are taken from. If you don’t have enough goods to cover the loss, they will take batteries to make up the difference. Once you are out of goods and batteries , they can’t take any more from you.

Losing Crew

Some adventures offer rewards that you can have only if you give up a certain number of crew members. Other adventures penalize you by taking a certain number of crew members. In either case, the game will zoom in on your ship and highlight your crew cabins, allowing you to select which crew members will be given up. Click a crew cabin to cycle through the possibilities. Aliens also count as crew members and can be given up if you choose. When you are satisfied with your choices, click the icon to indicate that you are ready to give up those crew members.

If you have no human crew remaining, you will have to give up on the flight once the adventure card is resolved.

Hits to Your Ship

Your ship will face many dangers. Some of these can actually knock components off your ship! Here is a list of things that can hit you and how you can defend against them:

  • A small meteor will just bounce off your ship unless it hits an exposed connector. In that case, you must spend 1 battery  to power a shield that protects that side or else the component will be destroyed.
  • A large meteor will destroy whatever it hits unless you shoot it with a cannon.
  • Light cannon fire can be absorbed by a shield. This will cost you 1 battery. Otherwise the component it hits will be destroyed.
  • Heavy cannon fire just destroys whatever it hits. You have no defense.

If your ship is hit (because you can’t or won’t protect it) the component that was hit is destroyed. This might cause some other components to become disconnected. They drift away into space.

If your ship is broken into two pieces, you decide which piece you will pilot to your destination by clicking it. The rest of the ship will be lost. Any components that are not attached to the ship when you are done with your flight count as components lost along the way.

Giving Up

You might have to give up before you reach your destination. Sometimes you might even choose to give up. This sort of thing happens all the time. Corp Inc. will tow you to one of their warehouses and let you try again.

If you give up:

  • You are just a spectator for the rest of the flight and none of the adventures can affect you.
  • When the flight is finished, you don’t get any bonuses.
  • Your goods sell at half price.
  • You still have to pay the penalty for the components you lost along the way.

If you are the only one left after everyone else has given up, you can keep going and face the remaining adventures alone. Cards like Combat Zone and Sabotage, which penalize a player for having the fewest of something, will be ignored. (You're not the worst. You're the best!)

Losing All Humans

You have to give up if an adventure card leaves you without any humans on board. (Aliens can’t run the ship by themselves.) This could be due to taking a hit that destroys your last occupied cabin, to losing crew after encountering a Combat Zone or Slavers, or to voluntarily sending your last crew member off on an Abandoned Ship. If you lose your last human in a Combat Zone, your ship remains in the flight until all effects of the card have been dealt with.

Open Space without Engines

Thanks to inertia, your ship can finish the journey even without engines as long as you don’t encounter Open Space. When confronted by an Open Space card, you have to give up if your engine strength is zero.

Choosing to Give Up

Sometimes, it might be better to give up and cut your losses. You may choose to give up by clicking your avatar to zoom in on your ship. Click the tab at the bottom of your screen to bring up the info panel and then click the "Give up on flight" button.

Journey's End

Once the last adventure card has been dealt with, the flight is over. Now it is time to collect your rewards! (And pay up for those components you lost along the way.)
