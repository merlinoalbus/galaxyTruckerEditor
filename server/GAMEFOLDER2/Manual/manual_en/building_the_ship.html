Building the Ship

Before you can fly across the Galaxy, you need to build your ship. You begin with one starting component and add new components as you go. Each must join with the components already in place. Your opponents are also building at the same time.

This page describes real-time building. Turn-based building is similar. The differences are described on a different page.

Ship Class

Some ships are bigger than others. The ship in the illustration above is a Class I ship, but you could also fly Class II or Class III ships, which are bigger. It all depends on the level of the flight. Higher level flights let you use bigger ships, but they have tougher adventure cards and more of them.

The squares show you the size and shape of a ship with the maximum possible number of components for this flight. Try to build a ship that fills most of these squares.

Revealing Components

Reveal a component by dragging it to your ship. You can attach it to your ship, return it to the heap, or save it for later.

Components you reveal will also be revealed to your opponents, and components they return to the heap will be revealed to you. Over time, more and more components in the heap will be face-up.

Attaching Components

Drag a component to your ship to see where it can go. All legal squares will be highlighted. When you drop the component on a legal square, it will rotate automatically to align its connectors with those already in place.

Click the newly placed component to rotate it. It will rotate only into legal orientations.

You can drag a newly placed component to a different legal square, or you can drag it back to the heap. If you release a component over an illegal square, it will return to where it was when you picked it up.

Welding

As soon as you drag a new component to your ship, the previous one gets welded in place. You cannot move it later.

Connectors

The game allows you to place new components only in certain positions and orientations. To understand these limitations, you need to understand connectors.

Each component has a functional part and 1 to 4 connectors. Connectors can be simple, double, or universal.


Any connector can join with another of the same type, and a universal connector can join with any type. A simple connector cannot join with a double connector.

Sides with no connectors are called smooth sides. Smooth sides cannot join with any connectors.



Each component you place must join with the rest of your space ship. If it touches the ship on multiple sides, all connectors must legally join. Two smooth sides may sit next to each other as long as the piece being added makes a legal connection on another side. Your ship must hold together at all times.

Don't worry. The game will not let you make an invalid connection.

The Well-Built Ship

Your ship should be solid, bristling with cannons and engines, full of cabins, batteries, and cargo holds, and completely protected by shields.

Exposed Connectors

Connectors that are not connected (i.e., those not adjacent to another component), are considered to be exposed.

Exposed connectors are allowed, but they increase the risk of damage to your ship by small meteors.



Exposed connectors can also slow your ship down due to Stardust.



The ends of exposed pipes get warped in transit, so Corporation Incorporated pays a bonus for the ship that arrives with the fewest exposed connectors. If you have a lot of exposed connectors, someone else will probably win this bonus.

Setting Components Aside

While building, you can have up to two components set aside. To set a component aside, drag it to one of the two squares on the left side of your ship. Once set aside, the component cannot be returned to the heap. You can add it to your ship at any time by dragging it to a legal square.

You want to attach set-aside components before time runs out. Any components that are still set aside at the end of building will count against you at the end of your flight. (They count as components lost during flight.)

Looking at Cards

During building, you may look at the adventure forecast. Looking at the cards can tell you how to build your ship for the given flight.

To look at cards, you must have added at least one component to your ship. Click a stack to look at its cards.

Click again to resume building. You may look at each stack as often as you like. However, you can't look at a stack that another player is currently viewing.

Of course, looking at the cards takes up time that could be spent grabbing components, but the information can be very helpful. For example, if you see a lot of planets, you might want more cargo holds.


Only 75% of the cards are available for preview. The rest will remain secret until the flight. Surprise!

Finishing Your Ship

Once you are happy with your ship (or once you run out of good places to add more components) you can finish building. You do not have to place a component on every square.

To finish building, simply drag a number tile from the top of the screen and drop it on your ship. The number tiles determine the order in which the space ships will launch. It is not enough to build a perfect space ship with lots of everything and no exposed connectors. You want to be the first! Usually.

In some cases, you might think it's better to be behind a few other ships (especially when your cannons are weak and theirs are strong). To take a tile other than the lowest-numbered one, click the stack of number tiles and all remaining numbers will be displayed. You can then take the number you want by dragging it to your ship.

The Timer

The timer measures how much time is left for building ships.

If no one takes a number tile before time runs out, the game will allow everyone to build for as long as they want. Once one player takes a number tile, the timer will flip over to give everyone else some final building time.

If someone has already taken a number tile, then the timer will flip over as soon as it runs out.

Once the timer has run out of time twice, building is done. No one is allowed to add any more components. Players who have not already done so must take numbers.

Preparing for Launch

If any players can take aliens aboard, the game will give them a chance to do so. If no one can take aliens, this step is skipped.

Building is done. Now it's time for the flight.
