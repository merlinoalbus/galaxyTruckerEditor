Alien Life Support Modules

The brown and purple components provide life support systems for aliens. In order for the life support module to have any effect, it must join with a cabin. This makes the cabin inhabitable by one alien of the corresponding color. Exception: you cannot put an alien in your starting component, the pilot cabin. Human astronauts don’t need any special life support components and can go in any cabin.

Players choose whether to take along aliens before ships launch.







Alien Abilities

Aliens are crew members and are counted as such in a Combat Zone or an Abandoned Station. You can send them away on an Abandoned Ship or give them up to Slavers. The disadvantage of taking an alien on board is that it takes up the space of two humans. But, of course, there are some advantages:

Purple aliens are a war-like species. If you have a purple alien, you get +2 to cannon strength. (If your cannon strength without the alien is 0, you don’t get this bonus. It’s not going to fight a space battle with its bare teeth.)

Brown aliens are good mechanics. If you have a brown alien, you get +2 to engine strength. (If your engine strength without the alien is 0, you don’t get this bonus. It’s not going to get out and push.)

Hits on Alien Life Support Modules

If your ship loses a life support module and it leaves an alien in a cabin that can no longer support it, the alien is lost. (It leaves the ship in an emergency escape pod, of course.)
