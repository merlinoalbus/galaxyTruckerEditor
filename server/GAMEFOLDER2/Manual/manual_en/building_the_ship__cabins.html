Cabins

Your starting component is a pilot cabin that can hold two astronauts. You can add additional cabins to your ship, allowing you to have a bigger crew. Bigger crews are better, so you want to have as many cabins as possible.



Advantages of a bigger crew:

  • You can send the crew to loot an Abandoned Station.
  • You can send crew members away in an Abandoned Ship.
  • You can afford to lose a few crew members to Slavers or Combat Zones.

If you have no human crew members left, you have to give up on the flight, so the more crew members the better.

To avoid losing crew members to Epidemics, you should try not to join two cabins together. But sometimes you just have to risk it.

Choosing Crew

When everyone is done building, each cabin, including your pilot cabin, is automatically assigned 2 human astronauts. If a cabin is highlighted, that means it can hold 1 alien instead. Click a highlighted cabin to cycle through its possible occupants. When you are satisfied with your crew, click the icon to indicate that you are ready for the flight.

Purple aliens give your ship +2 cannon strength (but only if your ship has cannons). Brown aliens give your ship +2 engine strength (but only if your ship has engines). They count as crew members for the adventure cards, but they are not human crew members. If your ship has no human crew members left, you must give up on the flight, even if aliens are still aboard.

These are the rules the game uses to determine which cabins can have aliens:

  • Your starting component cannot hold aliens.
  • Any other cabin can hold aliens only if it is directly joined to an alien life support module.
  • A cabin joined to an alien life support module can have 1 alien of that color.
  • A cabin joined to multiple alien life support modules can have 1 alien of any of those colors.

Your ship cannot have more than 1 alien of each color. If you select a purple alien in one cabin and then select a purple alien in a second cabin, the game will replace the one in the first cabin with two human astronauts.
