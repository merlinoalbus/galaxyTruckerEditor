Engines

Engines make your ship go. You don't need them all the time, but when you need them, you want to have a lot of them.

Engines have certain restrictions that other components don't have: The exhaust must point toward the rear of the ship. No component can sit on the square behind the engine.

The game handles these restrictions automatically. When you place an engine, it will rotate to point to the rear. Clickping on it will not rotate it because it has only one valid orientation. When an engine is welded in place, the square behind it will fade to red to remind you that no component can ever be placed there.


The best place to put engines is on the bottom squares of your ship. Ships with more engines are faster, so you want as many engines as possible. You will need your engines if you encounter Open Space. If you have no engine strength in Open Space, you will have to give up on the flight. Also, you don't want to be the slowest player in a Combat Zone.

Double Engines

Double engines have the same restrictions as regular engines. They are twice as powerful, and using a double engine requires spending one battery .

Don't forget the batteries!

Engine Strength

When you encounter Open Space or a Combat Zone, you will be required to specify your engine strength. Simple engines will be powered automatically. They don't use batteries. Any bonus from a brown alien will be applied. Beside your avatar, you will see your current engine strength.

You must click one battery  for each double engine you want to power up. If you change your mind, click the double engine to return the battery to its battery component. When you have chosen your engine strength, click the icon to indicate that you are ready.

This is how engine strength is calculated:


Each simple engine counts as 1.

Each double engine on which you spend a battery  counts as 2.

A brown alien adds 2 to your engine strength, but only if your ship already has engine strength at least 1. (He's a technician. He's not going to get out and push.)
